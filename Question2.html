The Bosnian Genocide

What is ethnic cleansing, and why could we call it that instead of genocide? What happens if we do? What are the implications of such discourse? What do the courts call what happened here (and why)?

Ethnic cleansing

Ethnic cleansing is the assimilation/removal of ethnic/racial/religious groups from an area attempting to make the ethnically homogenous. Ethnic cleansing is the expulsion of a group in a certain area, while genocide is the elimination of an entire group.


We call it “ethnic cleansing” rather than genocide because although it is the elimination of a group, the goal is to “cleanse” the area of the minor religions/races, which is essentially a more specific term for genocide if the intentions are to destroy the group. The implications of this discourse matter because of the difference between genocides and ethnic cleansing.


Calling the Bosnian genocide “ethnic cleansing” would make the massacre seem less severe than using the term “genocide”. In 2007, ICJ concluded that the Bosnian Genocide case was a genocide. They noted that there was much evidence of murder, rape, and torture. They decided that Serbia had not committed the genocide, but they failed to prevent it in Srebrenica.
